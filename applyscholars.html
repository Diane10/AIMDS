Young Scholars Programme

Overview of African Young Scholars Programme

African Young Scholars Programme of the African Institute of Management, Technology and Development Studies is open to Masters degree holders wishing to contribute to academic activities at AIMTDS and partner institutions. This is an exciting opportunity for Young academics with passion for international exposure to contribute to building capacity in Africa. The programme particularly provides opportunity for Young African scholars to spend one to two years in the Institute. This is an opportunity to contribute to shaping our future and building a world class institution in Africa. The Programme will support AIMTDS work by carrying out research projects, assitantship and writing reports. The Programme will give recent Masters Graduates an opportunity to utilize the resources and facilities at AIMTDS and to work with Institute Professors, Fellows, Researchers in areas of mutual interest. This is an opportunity to spend at least one year at AIMTDS utilizing your existing skills whilst developing new ones. You will be working full time during the programme. This may be the opportunity to link your postgraduate studies with a specific project that will benefit AIMTDS. A candidate whose application is successful will be granted affiliation as a Junior Research Fellow during his/her duration of stay at the Institute. Application from scholars on sabbatical leave from home institution is highly welcome.

African Young Scholars Programme

Duties and Responsibilities:

1. The jobholder will be helping AIMTDS in its mission to foster sustainable growth and development of the African and global development
2. Assist Senior Fellows and Professors in advancing the role of research in policy processes.
3. Engage in writing reports and grant writing,
4. Work with other AIMTDS research staff in on-going projects.
5. Engage in assisting Professors in teaching, training and consultancy at all levels using relevant methods, e.g. lectures, seminars, tutorials, etc, working as part of the academic team.
6. Assist in facilitating/organising capacity-building/development workshops, etc.
7. Help in curriculum development/course design and /or expand curriculum in emerging disciplines.
8. Participate in the Institute conferences

Qualifications, Skills and Experience

Applicants are expected to satisfy the following criteria when they submit their applications. The applicant must:

1. Hold a Masters degree or equivalent from a reputable university.
2. Be not more than 45 years
3. Have the ability to fulfill the objectives proposed
4. Fluent in oral and written English or French
5. Have proven interest in teaching and research
6. Possess very good quantitative, analytical and qualitative skills
7. Posses very good computer and IT skills
8. Have teamwork spirit and skills to work with people of diverse culture

Application

Application An application should include the following documents:
• A Curriculum Vitae
• Certified Copy of a Masters degree
• Data page of International Passport valid for at least one year
• A statement of the purpose/motivation
• A proposed work schedule
• Two Letters of Reference

Programme Benefit

Successful Candidates receive mentorship and gain an insight into the practices of conducting policy oriented research and training. The jobholder will receive a monthly stipend to cover transport and basic expenses throughout the duration of their stay at the Institute or any other partner institution.Fellows benefit from the educational resources and network of the Institute. NB:Please note as the African Young Scholars position is not part of the regular AIMTDS staff structure, Fellows will not receive the benefits provided to regular staff.

How to Apply

Application deadlines are as follows: 31st of March and 30th of September each year. Applicants should note that it might take up to three months before fellowship starts.
